Passive Cell Balancing & Low-Power Monitoring

Developed production-oriented balancing and low-power monitoring firmware for an EV Battery Management System built on the NXP S32K322. The work focused on improving cell voltage uniformity during charge and sleep conditions while keeping critical battery measurements active in reduced-power states.

Overview

This project addressed two tightly coupled BMS requirements: maintaining cell balance and preserving battery visibility during low-power operation. I implemented passive balancing logic using threshold-based decision making and PWM-controlled bleed operation, then supported low-power monitoring using FS26-based power management and low-power cell monitoring features from the battery monitoring ICs.

The result was a practical embedded solution that improved pack balancing performance without excessive thermal stress and enabled continued battery supervision during standby conditions. This work was part of a production-focused automotive BMS program with strong emphasis on reliability, diagnostics, and safe state transitions.

Key Contributions

  • Implemented passive cell balancing logic based on cell voltage delta and operating conditions.
  • Used PWM-based balancing control to limit resistor heating during extended balancing windows.
  • Enabled balancing operation in both charge mode and low-power or sleep mode.
  • Worked on FS26 PMIC-based BMS power management for reduced-power system behavior.
  • Supported low-power cell voltage and temperature monitoring while the system remained in standby.
  • Contributed to validation and debug activities for production-oriented BMS behavior.

Technical Details

The balancing algorithm identified higher-voltage cells and selectively discharged them through passive balancing paths. Instead of applying continuous bleed, PWM control was used to better manage thermal loading and provide safer long-duration balancing behavior.

On the low-power side, the design allowed the BMS to continue monitoring important parameters such as cell voltage and temperature without keeping the full controller stack fully active. This reduced unnecessary power draw while preserving the ability to observe battery health in standby conditions.

Engineering Challenges

  • Maintaining balancing effectiveness while controlling thermal rise in bleed resistors.
  • Reducing standby power without losing visibility of critical cell conditions.
  • Coordinating low-power transitions between the monitoring ICs, PMIC, and controller.
  • Designing behavior suitable for production firmware and automotive validation workflows.

Stack

Embedded C, NXP S32K322, ADBMS6832, ADBMS2950, FS26 PMIC, SPI, CAN, Vector CANoe, CAPL, oscilloscope-based debugging, automotive BMS firmware development.

Impact

  • Reduced cell delta voltage by about 10 to 20 mV during charge-cycle balancing.
  • Achieved below 10 mV delta in sleep-mode balancing scenarios.
  • Improved low-power monitoring capability for standby operation in the BMS architecture.
  • Supported production-oriented EV BMS firmware development and validation activities.
